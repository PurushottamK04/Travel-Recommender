Some famous places

Hawamahal

The Hawa Mahal is a palace in the city of Jaipur, Rajasthan, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it is on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur, and extends to the Zenana, or women's chambers.

Golden Temple Amritsar

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest Gurdwara and one of the most revered places of worship for Sikhs around the world. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, India, it is not only a spiritual center but also a symbol of human brotherhood and equality.

LEARN MORE
The Jagannath Temple

is the main attraction of Puri and one of the most sacred Hindu temples in India. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath (an incarnation of Lord Vishnu), the temple is known for its towering spire and intricate architecture. The temple complex also includes smaller shrines dedicated to Lord Balabhadra (Balarama) and Devi Subhadra (sister of Lord Krishna).

LEARN MORE
Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir is a partially constructed Hindu temple complex in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India. Many Hindus believe that it is located at the site of Ram Janmabhoomi, the mythical birthplace of Rama, a principal deity of Hinduism. The temple was inaugurated on 22 January 2024 after a prana pratishtha (consecration) ceremony.

LEARN MORE
Ganga ghat Varansi

Visiting the Ganga ghats in Varanasi is an immersive experience that offers insight into the spiritual, cultural, and historical aspects of India's ancient traditions. It is a place where life and death, ritual and devotion, all converge along the sacred waters of the Ganga

Taj Mahal

he Taj Mahal complex is believed to have been completed in its entirety in 1653 at a cost estimated at the time to be around ₹32 million, which in 2023 would be approximately ₹35 billion (US$498 million).The construction project employed some 20,000 artisans under the guidance of a board of architects led by Ustad Ahmad Lahori

Brahma Sarovar Kurukshetra

Braham Sarovar, as the name suggests, is associated with Lord Brahma, the creator of the Universe. Taking dip in holy water of sarover during Solar Eclipse is considered equal to the merits of performing thousands of Asvamedha Yajnas.
